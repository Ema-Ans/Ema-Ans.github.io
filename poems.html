Let Me Drown

fragment · Aug 2025
Learning hurts.
It pulls me under.
Sweet at first, like citrus on the tongue,
then the peel turns bitter and I have to chew anyway.
In his office, the air is thin and holds its breath...
If I sink again, let it be far. Past grades and gazes and measured worth. Past rescue.

A Wish, A Hope

fragment · Dec 2022
I don't know if I should wish for you or hope for us.
They say hoping is better, because for hope to exist there needs to be a mere possibility of that thing happening...
But because you are my strongest wish, as if putting scales to one's wishes, I think you are heaved up to become a hope of mine.

The Old Kind of Way

fragment · May 2021 (est)
From those dreaded questions of my dad, to that gentlemanly bow and the kiss on my hand.
From that bouquet of roses, "Only for you, Miss", to those sheepish twirls in my pink sundress.
From catching ladybugs on our secret picnic, to eating those stale macrons — how tragic.
That's how I wanna fall for you, the old kinda way...

The Senior Friend

fragment · June 2016 (est)
The earthy scent of mud,
with the brown and red in harmony,
embellished her old apple orchard, which was part of her ancestry.
It was home to all the rose bushes she had planted,
and buried the sole-prints of her grandad's morning strolls...
It taught her unconditional loyalty in this world of unsolicited cruelty.
