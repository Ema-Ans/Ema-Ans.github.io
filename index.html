Training & Questions

My work sits at the intersection of computer vision and human-computer interaction, with a focus on multimodal and temporal modeling of human behavior. I study how people think, move, and make decisions, especially in high-skill settings like expert chess. In my current work, I build vision pipelines using both classical feature-based methods and modern deep learning models to track pose, identity, and interaction over time. I also design user studies and visualizations to interpret these behavioral patterns.

During my undergraduate work, I focused on speech and language from a machine learning and systems perspective. I worked on training and evaluating speech and audio language models, with an emphasis on compute-efficient pretraining and representation learning. In parallel, I worked on Arabic natural language processing in low-resource settings, studying robustness across prompts, domains, and linguistic variation.

entry // ongoing

I’ve always liked lifting the lid. Not just seeing the output, but asking what machinery is humming underneath. I don’t always have the patience to fully understand it, but I like knowing it’s there.

This site is not just a portfolio. It’s a place to put words somewhere quieter, in the hope that not too many people read them, but the right ones might.

I don’t have a clean label for myself. I like structure, but I resist boxes. I care about research, but there are papers I still can’t force myself to read.

Some days I want to understand multimodal models. Other days I want to learn every prehistoric period, or identify plants by sight like the characters I grew up reading. There’s never enough time to do all of it properly, so I move between them.

I write because I need to make sense of things. Sometimes it’s research notes, sometimes something closer to a poem. Not everything that sounds right means something, and I’ve made peace with that.

I also really like teaching. There’s a moment where something clicks for someone else, and it’s very quiet, but you can see it happen. I think I can spend the rest of my life chasing that.

Part of me wants to disappear into a hut somewhere and write. The other part wants to build, study, and stay in conversation with the world.

This website is the compromise.

CV-Based Chess Analytics (M.Phil. Thesis)

I combine livestream video, Stockfish-derived difficulty, and timing signals to study expert decision-making in chess. I built a behavior pipeline with 3D pose and mesh estimation, plus identity tracking. Then I model movement signatures over time.

[under revision]

Prompt-Agnostic Arabic Automated Essay Scoring

I built a cross-prompt evaluation setup over QAES and CAST. I implemented PAIR-Net with prompt-conditioned cross-attention and caching, and ALiF-Net for Arabic linguistic features. Then I ran augmentation experiments to push agreement closer to human scoring.

NLPArabicEvaluationDeep Learning
Preprint Code

[published]

Thermal Feature Descriptors for Image Stitching (VCIP 2024)

Thermal images are noisy and unfriendly. I tested classic keypoint detectors and descriptors for thermal registration under domain shift, including SIFT, SURF, ORB, BRISK, and AKAZE. The result is a practical benchmark with clear winners, published at IEEE VCIP 2024.

Speech/Audio LLM Pretraining (Senior Honors Thesis)

I explored resource-efficient pretraining strategies for speech language models. I prototyped compression-based representations using deep compression autoencoders. I also built synthetic supervision to model speech errors at scale.
