About

Field Notes

I’ve always liked lifting the lid. Not just seeing the output, but asking what machinery is humming underneath.

Lately that machinery looks like vision pipelines, pose tracking, and identity graphs. Sometimes it’s Arabic essays, where I’m less interested in the score and more interested in what the score thinks it sees. Other days it’s speech models, and the quiet obsession becomes efficiency. How much compute is enough? How much is waste?

I care about cognition. About the small physical signals that leak out when someone is thinking. A shoulder shift. A pause. A gaze that lingers half a second longer than expected. Chess is perfect for this. It pretends to be about pieces. It’s really about the mind.

We live in a very image-forward world. Scroll. Swipe. React. I’m more interested in what sits underneath that. Words. Attention. Reasoning. The invisible architecture of a thought.

If I could, I would skip the highlight reel and talk directly to the brain. Since I can’t, I design studies. I build models. I get as close as the data allows.

I also teach. There’s a specific moment I love — when something clicks for someone else. It’s quiet. Almost imperceptible. But you can see it.

CV-Based Chess Analytics (M.Phil. Thesis)

I combine livestream video, Stockfish-derived difficulty, and timing signals to study expert decision-making in chess. I built a behavior pipeline with 3D pose and mesh estimation, plus identity tracking. Then I model movement signatures over time.

Prompt-Agnostic Arabic Automated Essay Scoring

I built a cross-prompt evaluation setup over QAES and CAST. I implemented PAIR-Net with prompt-conditioned cross-attention and caching, and ALiF-Net for Arabic linguistic features. Then I ran augmentation experiments to push agreement closer to human scoring.

NLPArabicEvaluationDeep Learning
Preprint Code

Thermal Feature Descriptors for Image Stitching (VCIP 2024)

Thermal images are noisy and unfriendly. I tested classic keypoint detectors and descriptors for thermal registration under domain shift, including SIFT, SURF, ORB, BRISK, and AKAZE. The result is a practical benchmark with clear winners, published at IEEE VCIP 2024.

Speech/Audio LLM Pretraining (Senior Honors Thesis)

I explored resource-efficient pretraining strategies for speech language models. I prototyped compression-based representations using deep compression autoencoders. I also built synthetic supervision to model speech errors at scale.
